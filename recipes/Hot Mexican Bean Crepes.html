Hot Mexican Bean Crepes

Pinto beans, sweet corn, and shredded Monterrey Jack cheese are nestled in light, soft layers of dough. This recipe gets its unique kick from ground ginger and a potent dose of chili powder.

Ingredients

Crepes:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted

Filling:

  • 1 (14 ounce) can pinto beans, drained
  • 1 cup medium salsa (such as Que Pasa®)
  • 1 cup canned whole tomatoes
  • 1 cup drained whole kernel corn
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese

Steps

Step 1

  1. Combine flour, milk, water, eggs, and melted butter in a large bowl; beat with a wire whisk until crepe batter is free of lumps.

Step 2

  1. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter into the skillet; cook until bubbles form on the surface, 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and cook crepe for 1 minute on the second side. Transfer to a plate. Repeat with remaining crepe batter.

Step 3

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Step 4

  1. Pour pinto beans into a small saucepan over medium heat; cook until heated through, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Mash into a fine puree using a potato masher.

Step 5

  1. Combine salsa, tomatoes, corn, ginger, and chili powder in a large bowl. Mix in pureed beans. Spoon some of the bean mixture into the center of each crepe. Roll up crepes, tucking ends in neatly.

Step 6

  1. Place filled crepes seam-side down on the lined baking sheet. Sprinkle grated Monterey Jack cheese on top.

Step 7

  1. Bake in the preheated oven until cheese is melted, about 10 minutes.
